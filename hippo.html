Hippopotamus

Hippopotamus Overview

The hippopotomus is a large semiaquatic mammal that is generally herbivorous. They outwardly resemble massive hairless pigs, but have wide mouths housing large canine tusks. Despite their barrel-like builds, hippos are capable of sprinting up to 30 km/h. Their diet is mostly composed of grass and some aquatic plants, but there have been occasional sightings of hippos eating meat and some cannibalism. This was likely anomalous behavior as their composition isn't suited for digesting meat.

Hippopotamus Behavior

Unlike cattle and manatees, hippos are highly aggressive and unpredictable animals often regarded as one of the most dangerous animals in the world. They are fiercly territorial in the water, but less so on land. As a result of their large builds and aggressive tendencies, hippos are not usually preyed upon in the wild. Aside from the odd hippo falling to a number of crocodiles, hippos often kill or drive alligators out of their habitats.
