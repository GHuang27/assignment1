Red Panda

Red Panda Overview

Red pandas are primarily herbivorous animals about the size of a household cat. They have reddish-brown fur on their upper parts and blackish fur on their lower parts while sporting long shaggy tails and short front legs. Their diet consists mainly of bamboo, but they also eat birds, eggs, and insects. Red pandas often forage in trees eating bamboo in large quantities because they are poor at processing it despite bamboo being their primary source of food.

Red Panda Behavior

Red pandas are solitary creatures except during their mating seasons. They are mostly nocturnal creatures spending daylight hours sleeping and becoming active during the late afternoon. Sleeping involves stretching out on branches on hot days and curling up on cold ones. When threatened, red pandas will usually escape by climbing nearby rocks or trees. If escape is no longer an option they will attempt to intimidate the other party or defend themselves using their claws.

Unfortunately, red pandas have a high death rate in the wild and a low birth rate as well. Deforestation and poaching are major threats to their existence as well as capture as pets.
