Cattle

Cattle Overview

European cattle are large herbivores typically raised as livestock for the production of beef, veal, dairy products, and leather. Females are typically referred to as cows and the males, bulls. Cattle are quadrapedal animals with cloven hooves that originally have horns. Genetic selection has resulted in the widespread existence of hornless cattle that are now raised on farms. They possess a digestive system specialized for the consumption of plants with a stomach divided in to 4 compartments. Feeding involves swallowing the food unchewed until the creature finds a safe place to regurgitate the food and chew it thouroughly before swalling it again.

Cattle Behavior

Cattle behavior varies depending on whether it's a cow or bull. Cows are typically friendly and curious. Cows that deal with humans more regularly are less prone to agitation when interacted with. Negative experiences will impact their behavior as well, so cows who dealt with humans in the past in less than ideal circumstances are likely to be more hostile.

Bull behavior is generally hostile as they are highly aggressive by nature. While bull fighting often has a matador waving a red cape to provoke the bull, they are unable to actually see the colors of it and are triggered by the movement of the cape instead.

Cattle in general dislike surprises and change with a strong preference for routines and familiar faces. They are also highly social creatures so leaving them isolated will severely stress the animal.
