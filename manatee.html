Manatee

Manatee Overview

Manatees are large aquatice herbivores often referred to as sea cows. They can be up to 4 meters long and weigh roughly half a ton. The tails and flippers of manatees are paddle shaped and they often propel themselves slowly through the water. Their diet consists of a large variety of freshwater and saltwater plants. Adult manatees typically eat 10-15% of their own weight everyday resulting in them spending as much as 7 hours of their day just eating.

Manatee Behavior

Manatees are very similar in nature to cows. They are typically slow and curious creatures whose main cause of death is human interference. Some manatees bear numerous scars from boat propellers and many die from collisions with boats and ships. About half of the day is spent sleeping underwater with short breaks for air.

Unlike cows, manatees are relatively solitary creatures. They are seen together most frequently when a male follows a female or a mother is accompanied by their offspring.
